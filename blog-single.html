Preparing Your Hives for the Spring Nectar Flow

By Emma Davis • March 02, 2025

As the weather warms and the first dandelions push through the soil, your colonies will begin to rapidly expand. Proper management during this crucial window can mean the difference between a record honey harvest and a swarming colony.

1. Early Spring Inspections

Once temperatures consistently stay above 55°F (13°C) on sunny, calm days, it’s time for your first thorough inspection. You are looking for three main things: food stores, the presence of a laying queen, and disease.

If the hive feels light when you lift the back end, you need to feed them immediately. A 1:1 sugar syrup mimics early nectar and stimulates the queen to lay.

"Spring management is about staying one step ahead of the bees. If they feel crowded, they will swarm before the flow even begins."

2. Swarm Prevention

Swarming is the natural reproductive method for honey bees, but for a beekeeper, it means losing half your workforce right before they need to gather honey. To prevent this, you must provide space.

Check the brood nest. If the queen is running out of empty cells to lay eggs, you need to add another box or "checkerboard" empty frames between drawn ones.

3. Adding Supers

When the dandelion bloom is in full swing, the main nectar flow is usually right around the corner. Add your honey supers before you think you need to. A good rule of thumb: when the uppermost brood box is 70% drawn and covered with bees, it's time to super.
